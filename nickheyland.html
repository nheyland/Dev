How I got here

Software Engineer

Commercial Pilot

2013

8.3.2013

I started my journey to becoming a pilot. I washed dishes for a local diner, and using every penny I had, I paid for my flight lessons.

2015

8.10.2015

I came to the realization that I was not able to pay for flight lessons at such a young age. I later attended Bridgewater State Unviersity in August of 2015.

2016

3.8.2016

In the Spring of 2016 I received my first FAA certificate, my private pilots license. I later went on to receving my Instrument rating a few days after my brithday in December 2016.

2017

8.15.2017

After my instrument rating I went right into training for my Commerical-ASEL pilot certificate. In August of 2017, I received my new license, and begun my long journey of becoming a CFI

2018

1.1.2018

In 2018, my interest in software peaked. I had seen a project called the metar map which shows pilots different weather categories on a physical geographically accurate map. I used others code to build my own. I still have the original, but I can confirm, it is not wuite presentable. Little did I know I would later go on to write my own software for my map in 2020.

2018

5.9.2018

As 2018 flew by, I spent most days and nights studying for my Certified Flight Instructors License. In may 2018 I recevied my CFI-ASEL Licesne allowing me to teach students how to fly planes. One of my greatest acheievements ever.

2019

3.9.2019

2019 was one of the biggest years of my life. I spend hours of my day teaching student pilots, but also spent time studying to receiving both my CFII and my Commercial Multi License. I also graduated with Magna Cum Laude from Bridgewater State University. I later went on to teach at the school it all began, Southern Maine Aviation.

2020

3.11.2020

Just before the pandemic of 2020, I worked 7 days a week to hit my ATP requried minimums to meet my deadline to join Republic Airways as a First Officer on the E175. About one week into training, I was sent home, the company was slowing operations because the pandemic has had virtually shut down the aviation industry entierly. After one month of not flying, I couldn't just sit around anymore, and went back to teaching as soon as my flight school opened.

2020

3.20.2020

In 2020 the corona virus pandemic struck the world. I was one of many whos career was terminated because of this. In my spare time I went on to learn softawre development on my own.

2020

5.11.2020

After years of hard work, and suddeny my world coming to a stop, I realized I could never really give up flying. I continued and even to this day spend my weekends teaching student pilots, and my weekdays working at Foreflight LLC, one of the largest aviation software companies in the world.

2020

6.15.2020

After my interest peaked in software development, I became extremely curious about what I could do next. I was discouraged by the fact that I had an aviation degree, and had never taken any classes in software development whatsoever. Foreflight, an app and software that I had used for as long as I had been flying opened a position in Portland Maine for a QA Analyst. Without having any idea what this meant, I applied. Somehow I got the job. Even to this day I am still extremely greatful I have joined this company. I haven't been there long, but it certainly changed my life so far.

2020

10.31.2020

With my new found love for technology, and after spending all day looking at other peoples 'bugs' I decided to make the commitment to start a new journey to become a software developer. I enrolled in a coding bootcamp called the coding dojo. And took off writing my own code.

2021

2.29.2021

In 2021, although it is still early in the year, I have graduated from my coding bootcamp and officially can call myself a software engineer. I am proficient in Python and Javascript along with many other technologies, while actively working on picking up new languages as time goes on.

2021

3.10.2021

I received my Gold Seal CFI Certificate for having a 100% pass rate of my students passing their checkrides the first time.
